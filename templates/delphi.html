The Delphic Maxims

The Delphic Maxims are a series of 147 maxims, originally attributed to the divine Apollo himself, which were inscribed on the Temple of Apollo at Delphi. They represent the best available synthesis of Hellenic morality, philosophy and thought. The version we present here was preserved by Joanes Stobaeus, a scholar from the 5th century AD, and has been corroborated by various archeological discoveries of different partial copies of the maxims, the earliest of which dates to the 3rd century BC. Thus, these sayings are very likely to authentically represent those which were inscribed in the Delphic temple walls. Meditate and reflect on them deeply, for they contain a huge amount of wisdom.
